title: Create a Product Set
description: Step-by-step guide to creating a product set that displays curated products on your product images.
---

## Overview

Product Sets let you display a group of products on a product image for cross-selling and upselling. You choose a layout, target which product pages the set appears on, and select which products to show inside the set.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Product Sets">
    In the LinkSpot sidebar, click **Product Sets**. You'll see a list of your existing product sets.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a New Product Set">
    Click **Create product set**. The product set editor opens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a Layout">
    Select one of three layout options:
    - **Button & Popup** -- A button on the image that opens a popup with products
    - **Card Carousel** -- A horizontal carousel of product cards
    - **Links List** -- A list of product links on the image
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Target Products">
    Choose which product pages the set should appear on:
    - **All products** -- Every product page
    - **Specific collections** -- Only products in selected collections
    - **Specific products** -- Only hand-picked products
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Products to Display">
    Select the products you want to show inside the set. You can pick them manually from your catalog.

On Starter plans and above, you can also use **automatic product sets** to populate products using Shopify's Search & Discovery recommendations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Display Settings">
    Adjust the layout-specific settings such as colors, positioning, and card size. These can also be configured globally in **Settings > Product Sets**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to publish the product set. It will appear on the targeted product pages on your storefront.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
You can duplicate an existing product set to quickly create variations with different targeting or products.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="info">
The number of product sets you can create depends on your plan. Check the pricing page in the app for your current limits.
</Callout>
